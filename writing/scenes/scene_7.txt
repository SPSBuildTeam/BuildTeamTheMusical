Scene 7: TECH DUMP FOREST - NIGHT 2X:00

Stage begins empty.

NARRATOR
The Build Team ran. And ran. And ran. So great and terrible was SSINROK’s power that they ran until they could run no more. And kept running. 

NARRATOR
(Somber) There is no machine that works forever. Eventually, all tools, no matter how sturdy, will billow smoke and fail. Repair, of course, is always possible, our heroes know this well. But what happens when there is nobody to cure the doctor? Nobody to fix the repairer? The once-steadfast machine becomes the lowest of the Lowe, reduced to mere technology in a dump.

Narrator pauses. Protagonist, Assassin, Money Guy, and Scientist enter.

NARRATOR
And so, the Build Team found themselves in a dump of their very own.

Protagonist, Assassin, and Scientist sit down in a loose circle. Money Guy kicks the ground a little bit and turns away a bit.

SCIENTIST
(Forced cheerful) Okay, now that we’re all gathered here, I’d like to introduce you all to my new schematic, the super cancer ray! We might not be able to beat him, but what if he beats himself?


MONEY GUY
I don’t know, what if- what if he’s immune to cancer… Yeah I bet that’s it, he has to be! No wait, I bet he’s listening to us right now…

Money guy starts looking around frantically.

JOHN BERNOULLI
Ugh! Do you even hear how lame you sound right now? Sit down, we need to think of something REALISTIC soon.

Money guy sits down and rocks back and forth, terrified, for a little bit.

ASSASSIN
He’s seen our demos now, he knows how they work… but what if we use them in a different way?

Scientist locks in.

ASSASSIN
The Van de Graaff generator looks pretty heavy the rest of you could throw that and maybe something else at him. A new projectile motion demo!

SCIENTIST
I think you might be on to something, while he’s distracted you can sneak behind him and write bad reviews on ratemyprofessor.com!

MONEY GUY
Did you actually know that over 70% of all internet traffic goes through Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud, and Cloudflare. I bet they have contracts with the NSA or something, he’ll know what we’re up to…

ASSASSIN
But if he’s distracted by the demos… this might just work.

SCIENTIST
Don’t worry Money Guy, believe in the demos. They’ll get us through this.

MONEY GUY
I’ve just felt so lost recently, I-

John Bernoulli gets up pretty quickly and is angry.

JOHN BERNOULLI
I’m so done with you freakazoids! I can’t do this anymore! (Miming head speaking with hand and in falsetto) Ooh, I’m Build Team and I love demos so so much, oh Build Team demo do my homework, Build Team demo take my exam. BAH! And you call yourselves physicists? The only demo you need is the human mind!

Bernoulli storms off to one side of the stage, pacing. The rest of the cast sneaks off. John Bernoulli makes their way back to the center of the stage and takes a seat (preferably a bench or chair).

JOHN BERNOULLI
Physics demo Shmysics demo, I bet I could make something even cooler right here.

John Bernoulli begins digging around in the ground at the base of a nearby tech dump tree (a tech tree if you will).

JOHN BERNOULLI
Hah, I bet I can make something that poisons Ssinrok, takes away his money, and even lowers his rating on ratemyprofessor.com all on my own.

John Bernoulli begins taking random junk pieces and hitting them together.

JOHN BERNOULLI
Now if I can only find that magnetic monopole…

John Bernoulli suddenly drops his parts and kicks them. They start to walk away, but stop and turn on the spot.

JOHN BERNOULLI
Wait, did that part move like a heavy symmetric top, or was that just me?

John Bernoulli pauses, and then sighs and sits back down in the chair.

JOHN BERNOULLI
It’s no use…

John Bernoulli slumps over a bit and sits there a while.

NARRATOR
Little did John Bernoulli know, that part could be modeled as a symmetric heavy top. If he was interested, he could have read about it in chapter 9.6, page 260 of my book: Classical Mechanics. Furthermore, if John Bernoulli had looked closer, he would have seen that although unique, each of his companions were not the blockheads he saw them to be. The Assassin masterfully wielded their tricks, rooted in physics and mechanics. The Scientists’ ideas were dreamt up from exploration of fundamental theorems, and the Money Guy… well they were just the Money Guy.

John Bernoulli weakly raises their head and looks around.

JOHN BERNOULLI
Huh? Who said that? Is there someone there?

John Bernoulli gets up and checks behind and under their chair.

JOHN BERNOULLI
Hmmm, must’ve been the wind.

John Bernoulli stops and looks at the sky.

JOHN BERNOULLI
I think… no, I know what I need to do now.

John Bernoulli walks off to the right of the stage and the Assassin, Money Guy, and Scientist walk in from the left. They sit down in a sort of circle.

ASSASSIN
I’m not really sure what to do now, I’m not sure if we can set up a demo fast enough to beat Ssinrok and hold it off.

SCIENTIST
We might still be able to, but we’re not coming out of it unscathed, if only John Bernoulli just trusted my demos.

ASSASSIN
YOUR Demos?!? All they do is crash and burn. Maybe if Ssinrok came to accepted students day, we could have him try one of YOUR demos and he’d be hospitalized.

MONEY GUY
Hey! Without John Bernoulli, we just have us, right? I’ve learned now that teamwork isn’t a transaction, we just need to trust each other.

ASSASSIN
Sorry, you’re right, I guess I’m willing to try one of the Scientist’s ideas, but I WILL be checking it first.

SCIENTIST
I guess… we can work on the demos together.

Assassin, Scientist, and Money Guy all try to do a three-way high five (not sure what that’d look like).
John Bernoulli walks on stage to the trio carrying a chair and sets it down and sits on it.

JOHN BERNOULLI
I have a plan… and I’ll need all of you to get it to work.

John Bernoulli and the rest of them stand up.

SCIENTIST
So boss, what’s this big plan of yours?

John Bernoulli points to the right side of the stage.

JOHN BERNOULLI
Scientist, Assassin, go to that clearing and grab as many parts as you can carry, money guy, get your cash and prepare for some overdraft fees. It’s time to build, team.

(The part where John Bernoulli says “build, team” has to be super emphatic and slower than the rest of the sentence.)
